Overview

This module implements a 4-stage inverter-based digitally controlled delay line (DCDL). As shown in the figure, the design consists of a cascade of delay cells, where each delay cell is formed by two inverters to preserve signal polarity while introducing a fixed propagation delay.

The input signal A propagates through these delay cells, generating multiple delayed versions of the signal. A hierarchical multiplexer network, controlled by the 2-bit signal Q, selects one of these delayed taps to produce the output Y.
